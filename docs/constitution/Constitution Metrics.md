# Constitution Metrics

The Constitutional Metrics are the only data that can falsify or defend constitutional claims.

Engagement proxies such as app opens, sessions, notification clicks, or raw page views have no constitutional standing.

## Monthly Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Falsifies |
|---|---|---|
| **Staff explanation rate** | App-related staff interventions per 100 visits | Self-evidence, one decision, hospitality |
| **Return with the same people** | Percentage of return visits within 28 days that include at least one person from a prior session | Article I: Belonging Is the Product |
| **Time to first play** | Median time from signup to first check-in | Doorway clarity and intent-first navigation |
| **Invitation conversion** | Share links that become people who actually play | The social loop and invitation quality |
| **Ritual repeat** | Percentage of first-time joiners of a recurring session who attend again within four weeks | Habit as ritual, not mechanism |

## Rule

If a metric regresses, the question is constitutional:

Which article did we break, and in which release?

The answer belongs in review and release notes, not in a separate governance archive.
