# Precedent #0002

## Public Generosity

## Background

During rollout of Experience Wave 1 the invitation copy "Bli först – ta med en vän" was interpreted by a real customer as an actual promotional offer.

The customer publicly asked in the Pickla WhatsApp community if they could bring a friend for free.

Rather than correcting the customer, Pickla honoured the expectation and invited the friend's participation.

The wording in the product was then corrected.

## Observation

Reality showed that the copy created a different expectation than intended.

The customer reaction happened publicly.

The gesture also happened publicly.

## Decision

When a customer's interpretation is reasonable because of our own communication, Pickla prefers protecting trust over winning the argument.

First solve the customer's experience.

Then improve the product.

## Principle

Public generosity creates more trust than private discounts.

Unexpected acts of generosity create stronger community than perfectly optimized campaigns.

## Scope

This precedent applies only to:

- small customer experience gestures
- ambiguous communication created by Pickla
- low-cost discretionary decisions

It does NOT create a general policy of giving products away.

## Implementation Guidance

Future staff should be empowered to make similar low-cost decisions without unnecessary escalation.

Every such decision should be:

- intentional
- documented
- followed by fixing the underlying product or communication.

## Product Action

Replace the invitation copy "Bli först – ta med en vän" with clearer wording.

Prevent the same misunderstanding from happening again.

## Key Lesson

Reality has veto power.

Real customer behaviour is more valuable than internal assumptions.

Every important observation should improve both:

- the customer experience
- the product itself.
