# Decision Framework

Use this template for meaningful product decisions.

The goal is not ceremony.

The goal is to make decisions legible.

## Problem

What is wrong in human terms?

Avoid internal language unless it is necessary.

## Observation

What did we see?

Include concrete evidence:

- Customer behavior
- Staff workaround
- Support question
- Data
- Production issue
- Repeated confusion

## Customer Story

Describe the moment from the customer's or staff member's point of view.

Example:

"A Play+ member opens an activity and sees day-pass and Play upsells. They now have to wonder whether their membership applies."

## Alternatives

List the real options.

Include doing nothing.

For each option, describe what becomes simpler and what becomes harder.

## Decision

State the chosen direction clearly.

The decision should be specific enough that a future person can tell whether the work matched it.

## Constitution Principles Involved

Which principles are active here?

Examples:

- Belonging is the product
- Intent over inventory
- Trust the resolver
- Members are recognized
- Delete before adding
- One customer, one moment, one decision

## Expected Customer Impact

What should improve for customers or staff?

Use plain language:

- Fewer taps
- Less doubt
- Faster arrival
- Clearer price
- Better recognition
- Easier staff action

## Tradeoffs

What are we accepting?

Good decisions have costs.

Name them.

## Review Date

When should reality be checked?

Add the date or weekly review where this decision should be revisited.
