# Experience Roadmap

This roadmap describes how Pickla should feel as it evolves.

It does not describe engineering work.

It describes the customer and venue experience Pickla is moving toward.

## Arrival

Arrival should feel expected.

The customer should know:

- They are in the right place
- Pickla recognizes them if possible
- Their access is understood
- The next action is obvious

For staff, arrival should be visible without searching.

## Belonging

Belonging should become visible.

Customers should see people, groups, hosts, activities, and moments that make the venue feel alive.

The product should answer:

"Where do I fit today?"

## Recognition

Returning customers should feel known.

Members should see their value immediately.

Regular players should not be treated as anonymous buyers.

Recognition should reduce friction and increase warmth.

## Habits

Pickla should help good routines form naturally.

The product should make it easy to:

- Return
- Join the same people
- Try the next activity
- Invite someone
- Check in
- See what is happening next

Habit should come from usefulness, not pressure.

## Community

Community should be felt through people and presence.

The product should move beyond listings toward social proof that feels human:

- Who is joining
- Who is here
- Which groups are active
- What moments are becoming rituals

## Identity

Membership, participation, hosting, and contribution should become part of a customer's Pickla identity.

Identity is not a badge collection.

It is the feeling of:

"This is my place."

## Hospitality

Pickla should behave like a good host.

It should guide without nagging.
Recognize without being invasive.
Sell without pressure.
Resolve complexity without exposing it.

Hospitality is the standard for every customer-facing moment.

## The Direction

Pickla should evolve from:

Booking a court

To:

Joining a living place

To:

Belonging to a community

To:

Helping that community grow.
